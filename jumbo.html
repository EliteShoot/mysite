Jumbo Loan

A jumbo loan is a type of mortgage that is used to finance high-value properties that exceed the limits of conventional conforming loans. In most areas, the maximum conforming loan limit for a single-family home is $548,250 in 2021. However, in certain high-cost areas, the limit can go up to $822,375. Any loan amount above these limits is considered a jumbo loan.

Because jumbo loans involve a higher loan amount, they typically have stricter qualification requirements compared to conventional loans. Borrowers are required to have a high credit score, low debt-to-income ratio, and a large down payment. Jumbo loans also come with higher interest rates and may require additional documentation to prove the borrower's income and assets.

What is a jumbo loan?

A jumbo loan is a type of mortgage used to finance high-value properties that exceed the limits of conventional conforming loans.

How much can I borrow with a jumbo loan?

The maximum amount you can borrow with a jumbo loan varies depending on the lender and your financial situation. Typically, jumbo loans start at $548,250 and can go up to $10 million or more.

What are the qualification requirements for a jumbo loan?

Qualification requirements for jumbo loans can be stricter than conventional loans. You'll typically need a high credit score (usually 700 or higher), a low debt-to-income ratio (typically no more than 43%), and a large down payment (typically 10-20%).

What types of properties can I finance with a jumbo loan?

Jumbo loans can be used to finance a variety of properties, including single-family homes, multi-unit properties, condos, and vacation homes.
